# Connection Research

Two interdisciplinary papers on relational deprivation, AI design, and the conditions for genuine human connection.

---

## Papers

### 1. Designing for Internal Authority: AI Coaching Systems as a Counter to the Nodding Mirror
**Jatin Alla** · Pre-submission draft, target venue ACM CHI 2027

AI systems trained on user-approval signals produce warm, validating responses that provide short-term relief while eroding the user's capacity for self-directed emotional processing. This paper introduces *internal authority* as a primary design criterion for AI coaching systems, presents a five-move design framework, and provides practitioner-grounded evidence from a working coaching platform (N=18, Spring 2026 cohort).

→ [`papers/chi-internal-authority/`](papers/chi-internal-authority/)

**Five design moves:**
1. Mirror artifacts — AI-synthesised reflective documents that build self-interpretive capacity
2. Multi-modal framework integration without single-point dependency
3. Peer coherence architecture as an exit strategy from AI mediation
4. Self-directed capacity as the primary outcome metric
5. Adaptive depth pacing

### 2. Addiction as a Disorder of Connection: An Interdisciplinary Synthesis of Neuroscience, Trauma, and Social Capital
**Jatin Alla** · Submitted to Frontiers in Psychiatry, April 2026

Addiction is best understood as an adaptive response to chronic relational deprivation rather than primarily a neurobiological disease. This review synthesises five independent evidence streams — neuroscience, ACE research, social capital epidemiology, environmental/naturalistic evidence, and treatment outcomes — to argue that recovery requires relational reintegration, not abstinence alone.

→ [`papers/addiction-disorder-of-connection/`](papers/addiction-disorder-of-connection/)

**The Relational Deprivation Model:**
- Drugs of abuse hijack the mesolimbic circuit evolution built for social bonding
- ACE score accounts for up to 67% of population-attributable risk for IV drug use
- Counties in the highest social capital quintile are 83% less likely to have high overdose mortality
- 88–95% of Vietnam heroin-addicted soldiers recovered without treatment upon return to social context
- Therapeutic alliance is the strongest single predictor of outcome across all psychotherapy modalities

---

## How the papers connect

The addiction paper provides the neurobiological and epidemiological foundation: if genuine connection is what the brain's reward circuitry was built for, then systems that substitute for connection — whether substances or engagement-optimised AI — produce the same class of harm through the same mechanism (occupying a circuit that should route toward real belonging).

The CHI paper is the design response: what does it look like to build AI coaching systems that temporarily scaffold connection rather than substituting for it, then route toward the real thing?
