# Connection Research

**Jatin Alla & Shalini Verma** · Two interdisciplinary papers on relational deprivation, AI design, and the conditions for genuine human connection.

---

## Papers

### [Designing for Internal Authority: AI Coaching Systems as a Counter to the Nodding Mirror](papers/chi-internal-authority/paper.md)

`Pre-submission draft` · Target venue: ACM CHI 2027 · Jatin Alla, Shalini Verma

AI systems trained on user-approval signals produce warm, validating responses that provide short-term relief while eroding the user's capacity for self-directed emotional processing. This paper introduces *internal authority* as a primary design criterion for AI coaching systems, presents a five-move design framework, and provides practitioner-grounded evidence from a working coaching platform (N=18, Spring 2026 cohort).

**Five design moves:**
1. **Mirror artifacts** — AI-synthesised reflective documents that build self-interpretive capacity
2. **Multi-modal integration** — multiple complementary frameworks so no single one creates dependency
3. **Peer coherence architecture** — group sessions as the designed exit ramp from AI mediation
4. **Self-directed capacity as outcome metric** — measuring autonomy growth, not session frequency
5. **Adaptive depth pacing** — treating peak distress as a signal to reduce AI demand, not intensify it

> *The test for any AI emotional support system: does your system leave users more capable of living without it?*

### [Addiction as a Disorder of Connection: An Interdisciplinary Synthesis of Neuroscience, Trauma, and Social Capital](papers/addiction-disorder-of-connection/addiction-as-disorder-of-connection.pdf)

`Submitted to Frontiers in Psychiatry` · April 2026 · Jatin Alla

The neurobiological disease model of addiction fails to explain three empirical anomalies: high rates of natural recovery without treatment, the graded dose-response between relational trauma and addiction risk, and the dramatic effect of social environment on compulsive use. This review synthesises five independent evidence streams to argue that addiction is fundamentally a disorder of connection — an adaptive response to chronic relational deprivation.

**Five evidence streams:**
1. **Neuroscience** — drugs of abuse hijack the mesolimbic circuit evolution built for social bonding
2. **ACEs / trauma** — ACE score accounts for up to 67% of population-attributable risk for IV drug use
3. **Social capital** — high social capital counties are 83% less likely to fall in the high-overdose category
4. **Environmental / naturalistic** — Rat Park; 88–95% of Vietnam heroin-addicted soldiers recovered without treatment upon repatriation; Portugal decriminalisation
5. **Treatment outcomes** — therapeutic alliance is the strongest single predictor of outcome across all psychotherapy modalities

> *Recovery is not the removal of the solution but the provision of genuine belonging.*

## How the papers connect

The addiction paper provides the neurobiological foundation: the brain's reward circuitry was built for social bonding. Systems that substitute for connection — whether substances or engagement-optimised AI — occupy that circuit and produce the same class of harm through the same mechanism.

The CHI paper is the design response: what does an AI coaching system look like that temporarily scaffolds connection rather than substituting for it, then routes toward the real thing?
