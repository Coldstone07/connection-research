## 5 New MUST FIX Items (2 concordant) — All Applied

**A-C10-MF1 = B-MF11 (concordant — Methodologist + Benchmark Expert): Table 7 "(\*)†" undefined notation implies significance on primary test that does not clear α=0.05.**
The asterisk family of notation universally means p < 0.05. "(∗)" with or without parentheses will be read as a significance indicator by any reviewer scanning the table. But the primary (two-tailed) p ≈ 0.053 does not clear the threshold. Caught independently and concordantly by both reviewers.
Fix applied: "(\*)†" replaced with "marginal†" in Table 7 significance column. Table 7 footnote updated: "†Two-tailed p ≈ 0.053 is primary and does not reach α = 0.05... 'marginal' = primary test is at the boundary of conventional significance; this result should be treated as a directional pilot finding pending replication."

**B-MF10 (Benchmark Expert, elevated to MUST FIX): Abstract lacks boundary-significance qualifier for two-tailed p ≈ 0.053.**
The abstract reports "consistent with Fisher's exact test (two-tailed p ≈ 0.053; one-tailed p = 0.037)" without signaling that the primary test doesn't clear α = 0.05. A reader of the abstract alone cannot determine the inferential status of the headline statistical result.
Fix applied: Abstract parenthetical updated to "(two-tailed p ≈ 0.053, at the boundary of conventional significance; one-tailed p = 0.037 under the directional assumption from tier construction)."

**C10-MF1 (Skeptic): §6.3/§9 Fisher result framing conflates construct-validity confirmation with independent capability demonstration.**
When the directional prediction follows from the tier-label definition, confirming it by Fisher's test primarily validates that the tiers stratify difficulty as intended (construct validity), not that frontier models independently fail at hard developmental stage boundaries. §9 Conclusion presents this as a capability finding without this distinction.
Fix applied: §6.3 now contains a paragraph distinguishing (a) primary reading = construct-validity confirmation (tier stratification works as intended) from (b) secondary reading = capability inference (frontier models lack hard developmental reasoning). §9 updated to "primarily validating that the difficulty-tier stratification captures a genuine performance gradient, and secondarily suggesting this ceiling reflects a frontier model capability gap."

**C10-MF2 (Skeptic, concordant with A-C10-SF1): Abstract and §9 Conclusion wrote "MCIP ≈ MCCIP ≈ 0.93" conflating MCCIP (≈0.94) into ≈0.93.**
§6.3 and Table 5 correctly differentiate MCIP ≈ 0.93 and MCCIP ≈ 0.94. The abstract collapsed both to ≈0.93, introducing an arithmetic inaccuracy and obscuring the near-zero gap structure.
Fix applied: Abstract now reads "MCIP ≈ 0.93 vs. MCCIP ≈ 0.94 — a near-zero difference." §9 now reads "near-identical MCIP (≈0.93, n=6 errors) and MCCIP (≈0.94, n=25 correct predictions)."

**C10-MF3 (Skeptic): Benner/Hagberg attribution "who similarly map Fowler's and Peck's outer stages" may overstate what those works explicitly claim.**
"Who similarly map" asserts the cited works explicitly perform a Fowler-Peck structural equivalence mapping. If these works engage the frameworks independently without explicitly comparing them, the attribution misrepresents source material. The §3.2 Unity mapping operational bridge now stands on its own terms; the citation should be framed as corroboration, not as precedent for the same mapping operation.
Fix applied: Attribution reframed from "who similarly map Fowler's and Peck's outer stages onto a shared post-questioning universalising category" to "who independently arrive at a structurally similar characterisation of the outer stage of spiritual development as a post-questioning, integrative orientation — providing corroboration for the shared-criterion mapping without requiring those authors to have explicitly compared the two frameworks."
